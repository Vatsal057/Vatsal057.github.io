# PRODUCT.md — Vatsal Vaghasiya Portfolio

## Register
brand — the portfolio IS the product. A visitor's impression is the deliverable.

## Who
Vatsal Vaghasiya, MTech Data Science student (Ramaiah University), AI engineer in
training. 18 shipped projects, 2 first-author papers under review, 5 specced
projects in build.

## Audience (priority order)
1. AI/ML recruiters + hiring managers screening intern/new-grad candidates (10-second scan, skeptical, mobile-often).
2. Engineers who click through to GitHub (verify depth).
3. Peers/professors (credibility).

## Purpose
Convert a skeptical recruiter scan into a GitHub click or an email. Every design
decision optimizes for: proof over claims, honesty over polish-theater.

## Brand personality
"Lab notebook of a rigorous, honest builder." Three words: **measured, candid,
hand-made**. The failures are on the board on purpose. Numbers over adjectives.
The site itself behaves like its owner's engineering: evaluated, documented,
no unverifiable claims.

## Voice thread
"AI that knows when it doesn't know" — calibration, abstention, verifiable
privacy. Repeated in projects, papers, and roadmap.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS-portfolio template (hero gradient, identical card grids, badge walls).
- Scroll-jacked "immersive" portfolios that hide content behind motion.
- Claim-heavy AI portfolios with no runnable code behind them.

## Constraints
- Static GitHub Pages (no backend). Single-page + data-driven case pages.
- Existing committed identity: notebook/paper aesthetic, Fraunces + Inter +
  JetBrains Mono + Caveat, sage/amber/rose accents. Identity-preservation wins
  over greenfield reflex rules.
- Recruiter mode toggle must keep working: kills animation + companion.
- No fabricated visuals: never fake app screenshots; only honest artifacts
  (real terminal output, real metrics, hand-drawn-style diagrams).

## Success criteria
Recruiter path: land → grasp thesis <10s → one click to proof (GitHub/resume).
No dead links. Lighthouse-grade a11y. Works on a phone.
